# Communication Guardrails

## Summary Tone

The summary should explain what happened and why it mattered without turning into blame or speculation.

Prefer:

- observed facts
- user or customer impact
- the mitigation or recovery path

Avoid:

- finger-pointing
- guesses presented as facts
- emotionally loaded language

## Customer Impact

Keep customer impact specific enough to be useful, but narrow enough to avoid leaking internal customer detail without approval.

If the audience is broad, generalize customer references.

## Root Cause

Write root cause as a systems explanation, not a person explanation.

Good postmortems focus on:

- what failed
- why the system allowed it
- what will reduce recurrence

## Action Items

Action items should be concrete and owned.

They are stronger when they clarify:

- who owns the follow-up
- whether it is open, in progress, or complete
- when it is due, if known
