# Writing Rules

## Summary

The summary should help a reviewer answer three questions quickly:

- what is on fire right now
- what needs a product or engineering decision
- what follow-up work is already assigned

Keep the summary factual and compact.

## Hot Issues

Hot Issues should focus on urgency, not just raw severity labels.

Prefer bullets that make clear:

- why the issue matters
- who is affected
- what the next action is

## Needs Decision

Decision items should be easy to scan and easy to escalate.

If the action field already contains a clear decision request, preserve that wording rather than rewriting it into something vague.

## Audience Fit

For an engineering review:

- technical detail is usually useful
- ticket IDs and owner names are acceptable

For broader product or leadership review:

- keep implementation detail brief
- translate issue impact into business or user language
